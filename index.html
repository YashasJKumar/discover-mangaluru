The Gateway to Karnataka

Mangaluru, formerly known as Mangalore, is a picturesque coastal city in Karnataka that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and modern amenities. From its golden beaches to historic temples and delicious cuisine, Mangaluru promises an unforgettable experience for every traveler.

Must-Visit Places in Mangaluru

Panambur Beach

13 km from city center

One of the most popular beaches in Mangaluru known for its golden sands and water sports activities. Perfect for sunset views and family picnics.

View Details

Kadri Manjunath Temple

4 km from city center

A historic 10th-century temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, known for its bronze statue of Lokeshwara and beautiful architecture.

View Details

Sultan Battery

6 km from city center

A historic watchtower built by Tipu Sultan in black stones, located at the confluence of Gurupura River and Arabian Sea.

View Details

Tannirbhavi Beach

10 km from city center

A serene beach accessible by ferry or road, offering peaceful surroundings away from city crowds. Ideal for relaxation.

View Details

St. Aloysius Chapel

City center

Famous for its stunning frescoes and paintings that resemble the Sistine Chapel in Rome. A masterpiece of religious art.

View Details

Pilikula Nisargadhama

12 km from city center

A biological park with zoo, science center, lake, and golf course. Perfect for nature lovers and family outings.

View Details

Ullal Beach

15 km from city center

A beautiful beach known for its scenic beauty and the Summer Sands Beach Resort. Great for swimming and water sports.

View Details

Mangaladevi Temple

3 km from city center

The temple from which Mangaluru gets its name, dedicated to Goddess Mangaladevi. A significant pilgrimage site.

View Details

Someshwara Beach

8 km from city center

A clean and less crowded beach near the Someshwara Temple, offering beautiful views and peaceful atmosphere.

View Details

Kudroli Gokarnanatheshwara Temple

2 km from city center

It was consecrated by Narayana Guru. It is dedicated to Gokarnanatha, a form of Lord Shiva. This temple was built in 1912 by Adhyaksha HoigeBazar Coragappa.
